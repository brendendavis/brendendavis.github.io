Designer's
Statement

I am a multi-disciplinary visual artist and designer dedicated to crafting impactful, purpose-driven visuals. My practice is rooted in strategic storytelling, blending thoughtful concept development with striking aesthetics to engage and inspire across print, digital, and motion platforms. From distilling complex policy for civic organizations to building atmospheric identities for cultural institutions, I create cohesive visual experiences that resonate with intent and clarity.

01
Case Study — Spec Work

Book Jacket Design

Catwings — Ursula K. Le Guin
Design Intent Warm, illustration-forward, and grounded in Le Guin's blend of whimsy and gravity. A watercolor background and burnt orange palette create atmosphere without sentiment. Full jacket: front, spine, and back.
Classic American Ghost Stories — Deborah L. Downer
Design Intent Archival and atmospheric — layering photography and road map texture to evoke place and unease across 200 years of American ghost lore. Full jacket: front, spine, and back.
Objective

Two speculative redesigns exploring how typography, image, and color work together to signal a book's world before a single word is read. Both demonstrate an understanding of print production, typographic hierarchy, and the full jacket as a unified design object.
